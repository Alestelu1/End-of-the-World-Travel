Our Mission &
Editorial Philosophy

Mission

Why We Exist

End of the World Travel was created to document and share information about the southernmost regions of Chile, including Puerto Williams, Cape Horn, Tierra del Fuego, the Beagle Channel, and Antarctica. Our goal is to provide accessible information about destinations, geography, routes, landscapes, and travel experiences while highlighting places that are often overlooked in international travel coverage. This project is developed independently and continues to evolve through research, documentation, photography, and collaboration.

What We Believe

Editorial Values

01

Independence

Content is created independently. We do not currently publish sponsored content or paid placements.

02

Depth

We aim to provide context, geography, history, and practical information rather than short promotional descriptions.

03

Responsibility

The environments we write about are among the most fragile on Earth. We take seriously our responsibility not only to inform travelers, but to promote travel practices that minimize impact and respect these places for future generations.

04

Storytelling

Stories help explain destinations beyond maps and coordinates, connecting geography, culture, and travel experiences.

05

Accuracy

Information is reviewed against official sources, maps, research publications, and local reporting whenever available.

06

Beauty

Photography and design help readers better understand places, landscapes, and travel routes.

Our Territory

The World We Cover

Our editorial focus is narrow and intentional. We focus on Southern Chile, Tierra del Fuego, Cape Horn, Puerto Williams, and Antarctica.

Chile
Magallanes Region

Puerto Williams, Punta Arenas, Cape Horn, Beagle Channel, Strait of Magellan, Tierra del Fuego, Yendegaia National Park, Diego Ramírez Islands, and the hundreds of islands and channels of Chilean Patagonia.

Chile
Routes & Frontiers

The Vicuña-Yendegaia overland route, the Hardy Peninsula channels, the Brazo Noroeste of the Beagle Channel, and the remote western archipelagos of Chilean Patagonia accessible only by sea.

Antarctica
The Last Continent

The Antarctic Peninsula, South Shetland Islands, and the Drake Passage — all accessed via Chilean gateway ports of Puerto Williams and Punta Arenas.
