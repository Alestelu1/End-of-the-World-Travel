# Photography Guidelines

**Project:** End of the World Travel
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active Photography Policy

---

# Purpose

Photography is a core element of the editorial identity of End of the World Travel.

Images should document Southern Chile truthfully and respectfully.

Photography should complement the editorial content rather than serve as decoration.

---

# Editorial Philosophy

Every photograph should help readers better understand:

* Geography
* Landscape
* Communities
* Wildlife
* Maritime heritage
* Protected areas
* Cultural heritage

The goal is documentation, not promotion.

---

# Authenticity

Use only real photographs.

Do not publish AI-generated images as documentary representations of real places.

AI-generated images may only be used internally for:

* Design mockups
* UI concepts
* Layout planning

They must never appear as documentary content.

---

# Preferred Image Sources

Priority order:

1. Official Chilean institutions
2. Museums
3. Municipalities
4. CONAF
5. SERNATUR
6. INACH
7. Ruta de los Parques
8. Wikimedia Commons
9. Local photographers with permission
10. Contributors with written authorization

---

# Image Licensing

Only use images with clear licensing.

Examples:

* Public Domain
* Creative Commons
* Government publications
* Explicit written permission

Respect every license requirement.

---

# Credits

Every photograph should include:

Photographer

Source

License (when applicable)

Year (if available)

Credits should remain visible through:

* photo-credits.html
* PHOTO_CREDITS.md

---

# Image Selection

Prefer photographs that show:

Real weather

Natural colors

Authentic landscapes

Local architecture

Traditional vessels

Scientific facilities

Protected areas

Communities (when appropriate)

Avoid overly edited images.

---

# Respect for Communities

Never use photographs that:

Exploit local communities

Invade privacy

Misrepresent Indigenous cultures

Create stereotypes

Represent people without appropriate permission when required

Communities should always be portrayed respectfully.

---

# Wildlife

Wildlife photographs should encourage responsible observation.

Avoid images showing:

Animals being disturbed

Unsafe interactions

Artificial feeding

Captive wildlife presented as wild

Promote ethical wildlife observation.

---

# Landscape Photography

Priority subjects include:

Puerto Williams

Cape Horn

Tierra del Fuego

Beagle Channel

Strait of Magellan

Diego Ramírez Islands

Navarino Island

Yendegaia National Park

Punta Arenas

Puerto Toro

Carretera Austral

Ruta de los Parques

Protected areas of Southern Chile

---

# Historical Photography

Historical photographs are encouraged when they:

Provide historical context

Illustrate maritime heritage

Document exploration

Show infrastructure development

Respect copyright and licensing.

---

# Image Processing

Permitted:

Cropping

Exposure correction

White balance

Minor sharpening

Resizing

Compression

Not permitted:

Adding or removing landscape elements

Generating documentary scenes with AI

Manipulating historical photographs in misleading ways

Creating composite documentary images

---

# Accessibility

Every image must include meaningful alt text.

Alt text should describe:

The place

The landscape

The subject

Its editorial relevance

Avoid keyword stuffing.

---

# Performance

Images should be optimized.

Preferred formats:

AVIF

WebP

JPEG (when necessary)

Avoid unnecessarily large files.

Enable lazy loading where appropriate.

---

# Future Collaboration

As the project grows, priority should be given to collaborations with:

Local photographers

Museums

Conservation organizations

Scientific institutions

Regional archives

Contributors should receive proper credit.

---

# Future Image Library

The project should gradually build a curated image library covering:

Cities

Villages

National Parks

Protected Areas

Lighthouses

Museums

Trails

Maritime heritage

Wildlife

Glaciers

Mountains

Scientific stations

Infrastructure

---

# Editorial Principle

Photography should strengthen trust.

Readers should be confident that every published image represents a real place, documented responsibly, and credited transparently.

The objective is to create one of the most credible visual archives of Southern Chile and its southern territories.
