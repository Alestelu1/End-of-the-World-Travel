# Sources Policy

**Project:** End of the World Travel
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active Source Policy

---

# Purpose

This document defines the source hierarchy and verification standards for all editorial content published by End of the World Travel.

Every article, guide, destination page and editorial note should be based on reliable, verifiable and transparent sources.

Accuracy always takes priority over speed.

---

# Editorial Principle

Primary sources should always be preferred over secondary sources.

Whenever possible, information should come directly from the institution responsible for the topic.

---

# Source Hierarchy

## Tier 1 — Official Chilean Sources

Highest priority.

Examples:

* Government of Chile
* INE Chile
* INACH
* CONAF
* SERNATUR
* Armada de Chile
* Dirección Meteorológica de Chile
* Ministry of Environment
* Ministry of National Assets
* Regional Government of Magallanes
* Municipalities
* Chilean National Library

These sources should be considered authoritative whenever applicable.

---

## Tier 2 — Academic Sources

Examples:

* Universidad de Magallanes
* Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile
* Universidad de Chile
* Scientific journals
* Peer-reviewed publications

Academic sources should complement official information.

---

# Wikipedia Policy

Wikipedia may be used only:

* for orientation
* for terminology
* for finding primary references

Wikipedia should never be the final source for factual claims.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

AI systems are assistants.

They are not primary sources.

Never cite:

ChatGPT

Claude

Gemini

Perplexity

Copilot

as factual references.

Every AI-generated statement must be verified.

---

# Photography Sources

Preferred order:

Official institutions

Museums

Municipalities

Wikimedia Commons

Photographers with permission

Local collaborators

Every photograph must include proper attribution.

---

# Geographic Information

Whenever possible verify:

Coordinates

Administrative divisions

Protected areas

National Parks

Biosphere Reserves

Official names

Official spellings

Official maps

---

# Historical Information

Cross-check historical statements using multiple reliable sources.

Avoid simplified narratives.

Provide context whenever possible.

Respect Indigenous history.

Respect maritime history.

Respect scientific history.

---

# Indigenous Communities

Use terminology recognized by:

Government of Chile

Academic publications

Community organizations

Avoid outdated or inappropriate terminology.

Treat Indigenous peoples as living communities.

---

# Antarctica

Prioritize:

INACH

Chilean Antarctic Institute

Government of Chile

Scientific publications

SCAR

COMNAP

Avoid repeating common tourism marketing language.

---

# Statistics

Every statistic should include:

Source

Institution

Publication year

Never estimate.

Never invent numbers.

---

# Maps

Prefer:

Official Chilean maps

Military Geographic Institute

Government cartography

Protected area maps

Official park maps

---

# Future Partnerships

Commercial partners are never considered editorial sources.

Advertising should never influence factual content.

Sponsored content must always be identified.

---

# Verification Checklist

Before publishing, confirm:

✓ Geographic accuracy

✓ Administrative accuracy

✓ Historical accuracy

✓ Source attribution

✓ Image credits

✓ Respect for local communities

✓ Respect for protected areas

✓ Editorial neutrality
