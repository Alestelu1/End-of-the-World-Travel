# Editorial Style Guide

**Project:** End of the World Travel
**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Active Editorial Standard

---

# Editorial Mission

End of the World Travel is an independent editorial publication documenting Southern Chile, the Magallanes and Chilean Antarctic Region, and the surrounding sub-Antarctic territories.

The objective is to provide accurate, respectful and well-documented information about geography, history, culture, heritage, nature and responsible travel.

The publication is not a commercial travel agency, booking platform or promotional tourism website.

---

# Editorial Philosophy

Every article should help readers understand the region rather than simply encouraging them to visit.

Content should always prioritize:

* Geography
* History
* Natural heritage
* Cultural heritage
* Scientific importance
* Conservation
* Responsible travel

---

# Editorial Voice

Write as an editor, not as a marketer.

The publication should feel similar to:

* National Geographic
* Smithsonian
* BBC Earth
* Government geographic publications

Avoid influencer language.

Avoid sales language.

Avoid promotional slogans.

---

# Tone

Professional

Calm

Objective

Educational

Respectful

Documentary

Accessible for international readers.

---

# Writing Principles

Always explain context.

Describe places before recommending them.

Prefer facts over adjectives.

Explain why a place is important.

Never exaggerate.

Never invent stories.

Never speculate.

Keep paragraphs readable.

---

# Point of View

Use third person.

Never write:

"We discovered..."

"We recommend..."

"You must visit..."

Instead write:

"Puerto Williams serves as..."

"Cape Horn marks..."

"The region contains..."

---

# Geographic Accuracy

Geography always comes before tourism.

Every destination should explain:

Administrative location

Geographic setting

Natural environment

Historical significance

Connections to nearby places

Only then discuss visitor information.

---

# Historical Accuracy

Every historical statement should be verifiable.

Never simplify historical events.

When uncertainty exists, acknowledge it.

Respect Indigenous history.

Respect maritime history.

Respect scientific history.

---

# Indigenous Communities

Treat Indigenous peoples as living cultures.

Avoid stereotypes.

Avoid romanticization.

Provide factual historical context.

Use official names whenever possible.

Examples include:

* Yaghan (Yámana)
* Kawésqar
* Selk'nam

---

# Responsible Travel

Travel should never be presented as unrestricted consumption.

Encourage:

Respect for local communities

Respect for wildlife

Respect for protected areas

Leave No Trace principles

Protected area regulations

Official park guidance

---

# Sources Priority

Always prioritize official Chilean sources.

Preferred order:

1. Government of Chile
2. INE Chile
3. INACH
4. CONAF
5. SERNATUR
6. Armada de Chile
7. Ministry of Environment
8. Ministry of National Assets
9. Regional Government
10. Municipalities
11. Museums
12. Universities
13. UNESCO
14. Scientific publications
15. IAATO (when relevant)
16. Reputable local organizations

International sources should complement—not replace—official Chilean information.

---

# Photography

Use only real photographs.

Never generate documentary images with AI.

Credit every image.

Prefer:

Wikimedia Commons

Official institutions

Museums

Photographers with permission

Local collaborators

---

# Style Rules

Prefer short sentences.

Avoid unnecessary adjectives.

Avoid excessive superlatives.

Use metric units.

Use official place names.

Include Spanish names when appropriate.

Example:

Cape Horn (Cabo de Hornos)

Beagle Channel (Canal Beagle)

---

# SEO Philosophy

SEO should never reduce editorial quality.

Write for readers first.

Search engines second.

Entities should appear naturally.

Avoid keyword stuffing.

---

# Editorial Independence

The publication remains independent.

Future partnerships must never influence factual content.

Sponsored content must always be clearly identified.

Editorial integrity comes first.

---

# AI Guidelines

Every AI working on this project must follow these rules.

Never invent facts.

Never fabricate statistics.

Never replace real images with AI-generated images.

Never introduce marketing language.

Never rewrite content without preserving editorial tone.

Never remove citations without reason.

Always preserve factual accuracy.

---

# Long-Term Vision

End of the World Travel is intended to become a long-term documentary reference for Southern Chile.

Growth should be gradual.

Every new destination should improve the quality of the publication.

The goal is not to become the largest travel website.

The goal is to become one of the most trusted editorial references for Chile's southern territories.

Every future contribution should preserve:

* Simplicity
* Authenticity
* Editorial independence
* Geographic accuracy
* Respect for local communities
* Respect for nature
* Respect for Chilean heritage
