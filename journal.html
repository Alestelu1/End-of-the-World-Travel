The Drake Passage: Ocean Corridor Between South America and Antarctica

The Drake Passage forms the ocean corridor between the southern tip of South America and the Antarctic Peninsula. From Chile's southern territories, it connects the region of Cape Horn, Puerto Williams, and the Diego Ramírez Islands with the wider Southern Ocean and Antarctic routes.

Beyond its reputation for rough seas, the Drake Passage is one of the most important oceanographic regions on Earth. The Antarctic Circumpolar Current flows through this corridor, influencing climate, marine ecosystems, scientific research, and Antarctic navigation.

The passage is also linked to the protected marine and subantarctic environments of the Diego Ramírez and Drake Passage National Park, one of the largest protected areas under Chilean administration in the far south.
